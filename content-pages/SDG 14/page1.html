What is SDG 14 - Life Below Water?

Sustainable Development Goal 14 (SDG 14) focuses on "Life Below Water." This goal aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. SDG 14 targets a range of issues such as marine pollution, overfishing, habitat destruction, and the impacts of climate change on marine ecosystems. It calls for the protection and restoration of marine and coastal ecosystems, the regulation of fishing practices to ensure sustainability, and the reduction of marine pollution from land-based activities. Achieving SDG 14 is crucial for safeguarding marine biodiversity, ensuring the livelihoods of millions who depend on marine resources, and preserving the health and resilience of our planet's oceans for future generations.
