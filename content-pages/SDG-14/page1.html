What is SDG 14 - Life Below Water?

Sustainable Development Goal 14 (SDG 14) stands as a beacon for safeguarding the life below water, an essential element of our planet's ecosystem. With oceans covering over 70% of the Earth's surface, they are the lifeblood of our planet, regulating climate, providing sustenance, and nurturing diverse ecosystems. Yet, the relentless march of human activity, from overfishing to pollution, coastal development, and climate change, has cast a shadow over the health and biodiversity of marine environments worldwide.

SDG 14 calls for urgent action to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources. Central to this goal is the preservation and restoration of marine ecosystems, including vital habitats like coral reefs and mangroves. These ecosystems not only support a plethora of marine life but also serve as natural buffers against coastal erosion and storm surges, providing invaluable services to coastal communities.

At the heart of SDG 14 lies the imperative to ensure the sustainable management of fisheries. Overfishing has depleted fish stocks, threatening the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on marine resources for food and income. By adopting sustainable fishing practices, such as regulating catch quotas, combating illegal fishing, and promoting ecosystem-based management, we can ensure the long-term viability of fisheries while preserving marine biodiversity.
